Jean-Louis Faure

The great oncologist


Tribute to a famous and respected French oncologist who revolutionized abdominal hysterectomy techniques, providing opportunities for research of cancer surgery and improving patient survival.

Timeline of Dr. Jean-Louis Faure

  • 1863 - Jean-Louis Faure was born in Sainte-Foy-la Grande town in France.
  • 1884 - Enrolled in the Faculty of Medicine
  • 1886 - Faure became an extern in the department of Alexis Legroux
  • 1887 - Faure becomes an intern
  • 1888 - Faure married Madeleine Bourgeois. Had four children from this marriage
  • 1889 - He was appointed as an assistant in anatomy department and later as a lecturer
  • 1895 - Became hospital physician
  • 1898 - He obtained the title of associate professor of surgery
  • 1899 - He was appointed lecturer of the surgical clinic at the Hotel-Dieu hospital
  • 1918 - He was designated lecturer of clinical gynecology and later replaced the late professor Samuel Pozzi in the chair of clinical gynecology at Broca Hospital
  • 1924 - Faure received the insignia of Commander of the Legion of Honor and became a member of the French Academy of Medicine. One year after that he was elected as the president of the Surgical Society
  • 1934 - He became emeritus professor of surgery

Publications

  • 1892 - The suspensory apparatus of the liver, hepatoptosis and hepatopexy
  • 1902 - Surgery of the uterine adnexa.
  • 1906 - Book of hysterectomy
  • 1911 - Treatise on medical and surgical gynecology.
